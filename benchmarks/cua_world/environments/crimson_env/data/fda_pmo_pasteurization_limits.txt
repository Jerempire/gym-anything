FDA GRADE A PASTEURIZED MILK ORDINANCE (PMO) - 2023 REVISION
21 CFR PART 131 - MILK AND CREAM - PASTEURIZATION REQUIREMENTS
HTST (HIGH TEMPERATURE SHORT TIME) PASTEURIZATION CRITICAL CONTROL POINTS
Source: U.S. Food and Drug Administration / National Conference on Interstate Milk Shipments (NCIMS)

=== FDA PMO CRITICAL LIMITS FOR SCADA TAG CONFIGURATION ===
(HTST pasteurizer - continuous flow system, dairy products)

TAG: TT_501 (HTST Holder Temperature - CCP1)
  Engineering Unit Label: Degrees Fahrenheit
  Low Alarm Threshold : 161.0  (FDA PMO CRITICAL LIMIT for HTST - 161F for 15 seconds kills pathogens; below this triggers flow diversion)
  High Alarm Threshold: 185.0  (maximum holder temperature - above this causes protein denaturation and fouling)

TAG: FT_501 (HTST Forward Flow Rate)
  Engineering Unit Label: Gallons per Minute
  Low Alarm Threshold : 5.0    (minimum flow to maintain turbulent conditions in heat exchanger)
  High Alarm Threshold: 300.0  (maximum rated capacity - above this reduces hold time below 15 seconds)

TAG: PT_501 (Pasteurized Product Pressure)
  Engineering Unit Label: Pounds per Square Inch
  Low Alarm Threshold : 1.0    (FDA PMO CRITICAL: pasteurized side must maintain higher pressure than raw side to prevent contamination cross-flow; must be > 0 psi differential)
  High Alarm Threshold: 80.0   (maximum operating pressure for heat exchanger gaskets per ASME BPE-2019)

TAG: TT_502 (Regeneration Balance Temperature)
  Engineering Unit Label: Degrees Fahrenheit
  Low Alarm Threshold : 130.0  (minimum balance tank temperature - below this indicates thermal loss in regeneration section)
  High Alarm Threshold: 185.0  (maximum regeneration outlet temperature - above this causes excessive steam consumption)

TAG: FQ_501 (Daily Batch Total Volume)
  Engineering Unit Label: Gallons
  Low Alarm Threshold : 0.0    (daily totalizer - 0 is reset at start of shift)
  High Alarm Threshold: 900000.0 (production cap per daily operating permit - 900,000 gallons/day maximum)

=== CONFIGURATION NOTES ===
- All tags use Float data type (continuous process measurements)
- Alarm 1 (Low) triggers when value falls below threshold (Event Mode: High to Low)
- Alarm 2 (High) triggers when value rises above threshold (Event Mode: Low to High)
- TT_501 alarms are CRITICAL CONTROL POINTS under HACCP - must be configured first
