=== ALARM THRESHOLDS FOR SCADA TAG CONFIGURATION ===

TAG: FT_201 (Gas Flow Rate)
  Engineering Unit Label: Million Std Cubic Feet per Day
  Low Alarm Threshold : 0.500  (AGA-3 minimum reliable measurement - below this, meter uncertainty exceeds spec)
  High Alarm Threshold: 8.500  (95% of rated capacity - sustained flow above this signals potential overproduction)

TAG: PT_201 (Upstream Static Pressure)
  Engineering Unit Label: Pounds per Sq Inch Absolute
  Low Alarm Threshold : 200.0  (minimum operating pressure for AGA-3 meter accuracy at full flow)
  High Alarm Threshold: 1200.0 (80% of pipeline MAOP - maximum allowable operating pressure)

TAG: TT_201 (Gas Temperature)
  Engineering Unit Label: Degrees Fahrenheit
  Low Alarm Threshold : 10.0   (hydrate formation risk below 32F at pipeline pressure; this margin allows response time)
  High Alarm Threshold: 130.0  (AGA-3 Table 1: compressibility factors validated to 130F; extrapolation above this introduces error)

TAG: DP_201 (Differential Pressure)
  Engineering Unit Label: Inches Water Column
  Low Alarm Threshold : 0.0    (zero DP indicates no flow or blocked impulse line)
  High Alarm Threshold: 200.0  (AGA-3 maximum recommended DP for orifice plates - beta ratio constraint)

TAG: AT_201 (Hydrogen Sulfide Concentration)
  Engineering Unit Label: Parts per Million
  Low Alarm Threshold : 0.0    (baseline; 0 ppm is normal - no low alarm action needed)
  High Alarm Threshold: 10.0   (OSHA PEL ceiling limit for H2S exposure; requires immediate evacuation above this level)

TAG: FQ_201 (Cumulative Gas Volume)
  Engineering Unit Label: Thousand Standard Cubic Feet
  Low Alarm Threshold : 0.0    (totalizer starts at 0 each day - no low alarm needed)
  High Alarm Threshold: 500000.0 (500 MMscfd daily production cap per operating agreement)
