WHO GUIDELINES FOR DRINKING-WATER QUALITY (4th Edition, 2022)
AWWA DISTRIBUTION SYSTEM REQUIREMENTS - OPERATIONAL ALARM THRESHOLDS
Source: World Health Organization / American Water Works Association

=== ALARM THRESHOLDS FOR SCADA TAG CONFIGURATION ===

TAG: CT_101 (Free Chlorine Residual)
  Engineering Unit Label: mg per Liter
  Low Alarm Threshold : 0.20  (WHO minimum residual for distribution - Chapter 9)
  High Alarm Threshold: 4.00  (WHO maximum for disinfection byproduct control)

TAG: PH_101 (Process Water pH)
  Engineering Unit Label: pH Units
  Low Alarm Threshold : 6.50  (WHO lower limit - corrosion risk below this value)
  High Alarm Threshold: 8.50  (WHO upper limit - effective chlorination requires pH below 8.5)

TAG: TU_101 (Turbidity)
  Engineering Unit Label: Nephelometric Turbidity Units
  Low Alarm Threshold : 0.00  (lower bound - no low alarm needed; 0 is physical minimum)
  High Alarm Threshold: 4.00  (WHO guideline value for treated water entering distribution)

TAG: TT_101 (Raw Water Temperature)
  Engineering Unit Label: Degrees Celsius
  Low Alarm Threshold : 1.00  (near-freezing; risk of freezing infrastructure)
  High Alarm Threshold: 32.00 (elevated temperature promotes microbial growth - AWWA M58)

TAG: PT_101 (Distribution Pressure)
  Engineering Unit Label: Kilopascals
  Low Alarm Threshold : 138.00 (AWWA C651 minimum - 20 psi; prevents backflow intrusion)
  High Alarm Threshold: 586.00 (AWWA design maximum - 85 psi; system overpressure risk)

=== CONFIGURATION NOTES ===
- All tags use Float data type (continuous analog signals from sensors)
- Alarm 1 (Low) should be configured to trigger when process value falls below threshold
  (Event Mode: High to Low)
- Alarm 2 (High) should be configured to trigger when process value rises above threshold
  (Event Mode: Low to High)
