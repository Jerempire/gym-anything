ANSI/BICSI 002-2019 - DATA CENTER DESIGN AND IMPLEMENTATION BEST PRACTICES
Source: American Society of Heating, Refrigerating and Air-Conditioning Engineers

=== ASHRAE A2 CLASS DATA CENTER - ALARM THRESHOLDS FOR SCADA TAG CONFIGURATION ===
(A2 class: Typical enterprise data center with some redundancy)

TAG: TT_401 (IT Equipment Inlet Temperature)
  Engineering Unit Label: Degrees Celsius
  Low Alarm Threshold : 10.0   (ASHRAE A2 lower recommended inlet temp - condensation risk below this)
  High Alarm Threshold: 35.0   (ASHRAE A2 upper allowable inlet temperature for IT equipment)

TAG: RH_401 (Relative Humidity)
  Engineering Unit Label: Percent Relative Humidity
  Low Alarm Threshold : 20.0   (ASHRAE lower limit - below 20% RH increases static electricity discharge risk)
  High Alarm Threshold: 80.0   (ASHRAE upper limit - above 80% RH causes condensation on circuit boards)

TAG: DP_401 (Dew Point Temperature)
  Engineering Unit Label: Degrees Celsius
  Low Alarm Threshold : -12.0  (ASHRAE minimum dew point for A2 class - related to static electricity risk)
  High Alarm Threshold: 17.0   (ASHRAE maximum dew point - moisture condensation risk above 17C dew point)

TAG: PT_401 (CRAC Discharge Static Pressure)
  Engineering Unit Label: Inches Water Gauge
  Low Alarm Threshold : 0.0    (zero or negative pressure indicates CRAC fan failure)
  High Alarm Threshold: 1.5    (maximum recommended raised floor plenum pressure per BICSI 002)

TAG: FT_401 (CRAC Unit Airflow Rate)
  Engineering Unit Label: Cubic Feet per Minute
  Low Alarm Threshold : 1000.0 (minimum airflow for cooling - below this indicates fan failure or blockage)
  High Alarm Threshold: 9000.0 (maximum rated airflow at 90% capacity - above suggests bypass airflow issue)

TAG: TT_402 (Return Air Temperature - Hot Aisle)
  Engineering Unit Label: Degrees Celsius
  Low Alarm Threshold : 20.0   (below 20C return air indicates over-cooling / cold air bypass)
  High Alarm Threshold: 45.0   (above 45C return air indicates hot aisle containment failure)

=== CONFIGURATION NOTES ===
- All tags use Float data type (continuous HVAC sensor analog signals)
- Alarm 1 (Low) triggers when value falls below threshold (Event Mode: High to Low)
- Alarm 2 (High) triggers when value rises above threshold (Event Mode: Low to High)
