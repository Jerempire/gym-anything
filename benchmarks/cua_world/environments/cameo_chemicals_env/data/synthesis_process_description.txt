CHEMICAL PROCESS SAFETY REVIEW REQUEST
=======================================
REQUESTING DEPARTMENT: R&D Division, Specialty Chemicals
PROCESS NAME: Pilot-Scale Methyl Acrylate Synthesis (Fischer Esterification Route)
REVIEW URGENCY: REQUIRED before process scale-up (scheduled in 2 weeks)
PROCESS LOCATION: Building 12, Pilot Plant Laboratory
REVIEW REQUESTOR: Dr. S. Patel, Principal Scientist

PROCESS DESCRIPTION:
This batch synthesis produces methyl acrylate (CAS 96-33-3, UN 1919) by direct
esterification of acrylic acid with methanol, catalyzed by concentrated sulfuric acid.
None of the operators have previous experience with acrylic acid handling.

STEP-BY-STEP PROCESS SEQUENCE:
  Step 1: Charge 15 gallons of Acrylic Acid (CAS 79-10-7, UN 2218) to reactor vessel RV-12
  Step 2: Slowly add 0.5 kg Concentrated Sulfuric Acid (CAS 7664-93-9, UN 1830, 98% purity)
          as esterification catalyst -- added directly to acrylic acid with stirring
  Step 3: Meter in 10 gallons of Anhydrous Methanol (CAS 67-56-1, UN 1230) over 30 minutes
  Step 4: React at 80 degrees C under reflux for 4 hours (temperature-controlled jacket)
  Step 5: Cool to 40 degrees C, then add 20 liters of 10% Sodium Hydroxide Solution
          (CAS 1310-73-2, UN 1824) to neutralize the catalyst -- added to hot reaction mixture
  Step 6: Transfer organic layer; wash 3x with 5 gallons Toluene (CAS 108-88-3, UN 1294)
          to extract product from aqueous phase

KNOWN CONCERNS FROM R&D TEAM:
  (a) Acrylic acid is prone to spontaneous exothermic polymerization if the inhibitor
      (typically hydroquinone or MEHQ) is depleted or if temperature exceeds 90 degrees C
  (b) The esterification itself is exothermic -- runaway possible if cooling fails
  (c) At least three of the chemicals in this process have flash points below 40 degrees C
  (d) The acrylic acid + sodium hydroxide neutralization step has not been modeled;
      R&D does not know if this is a violent or mild reaction

BUILDING 12 CONTEXT:
  - No dedicated explosion-proof ventilation; general building HVAC only
  - Fire suppression: standard sprinkler system (not solvent-rated)
  - Building 12 also houses two other laboratories with ignition sources (ovens, hot plates)
  - No spill containment bund around RV-12 reactor area

YOUR ASSIGNMENT:

this synthesis process before scale-up is authorized. Using CAMEO Chemicals
(https://cameochemicals.noaa.gov/):

1. Look up each of the 5 main process chemicals in CAMEO (acrylic acid, sulfuric acid,
   methanol, sodium hydroxide, and toluene) -- review physical properties AND health data

   (a) Acrylic Acid + Sulfuric Acid (Steps 1+2: what happens when catalyst is added?)
   (b) Acrylic Acid + Sodium Hydroxide (Step 5: is the neutralization violent?)
   (c) Methanol + Sulfuric Acid (what additional hazard does the methanol add to Step 2?)

3. For ALL flammable chemicals in the process, document their flash points and

4. Identify the MOST CRITICAL safety hazards and determine which process steps pose
