CEDAR RIVER INDUSTRIAL CORRIDOR — EMERGENCY HAZMAT ASSESSMENT
County Emergency Management Agency
Dispatch Reference: CEMA-2026-FL-0041
Date/Time: March 21, 2026, 14:30 EDT

================================================================================
SITUATION OVERVIEW
================================================================================

The National Weather Service has issued a Flash Flood Warning for the Cedar River
watershed. River stage is forecast to reach 28.4 feet (major flood stage = 24.0
feet) by 18:00 EDT. Three chemical storage facilities along the river corridor
are at risk. A cascading hazard assessment is needed before committing response
resources.

WEATHER CONDITIONS:
  Wind:        From NW at 8 mph (steady, gusting to 12 mph)
  Temperature: 72 deg F
  Humidity:    65%
  Sky:         Overcast with light intermittent rain
  Visibility:  4 miles (reduced by precipitation)

POPULATION AT RISK:
  - Cedar Heights residential subdivision: 2,400 residents, 1.5 miles SE
  - Cedar River Elementary School: 380 students + staff, 1.8 miles E
  - Facility Beta employees: 12 on site
  - Facility Gamma employees: 8 on site
  - Highway 9 average traffic: ~800 vehicles/hour (bisects corridor)

================================================================================
FACILITY ALPHA — Cedar River Industrial Park
  Address:   1200 River Road, River Mile 12.3
  Elevation: 22.1 feet (WILL BE INUNDATED at 24.0 feet flood stage)
================================================================================

Warehouse W-7: Floor-level storage on wooden pallets. No secondary containment
rated for flood events. Building is steel-frame with corrugated metal walls.
Overhead sprinkler system (water-based) may activate if pressure is maintained.

CHEMICAL INVENTORY:
  Bay A-1: Calcium Carbide
           Quantity: 2,000 kg in sealed steel drums (50 kg each, 40 drums)
           UN Number: 1402
           Packaging: Steel drums with welded lids, some showing corrosion

  Bay A-2: Sodium metal
           Quantity: 500 kg in kerosene-blanketed sealed drums
           UN Number: 1428
           Packaging: Steel drums under mineral oil/kerosene blanket

  Bay A-3: Phosphorus Trichloride
           Quantity: 800 kg in glass-lined steel containers
           UN Number: 1809
           Packaging: Glass-lined steel carboys with screw caps, 20 kg each

STATUS: Facility evacuated at 13:45 EDT. No personnel on site. Security cameras
show rising water in the parking lot. Floodwater is projected to reach the
warehouse floor (22.1 ft) within 3.5 hours. Once water enters, all three
chemical bays will be affected simultaneously.

CONCERN: If drums are breached or overturned by flood currents, all three
chemicals will be exposed to water. The overhead sprinkler system may compound
the situation if it activates.

  Address:   450 Industrial Boulevard, River Mile 10.2

================================================================================

Located 2.1 miles downstream (SE) of Facility Alpha and 1.3 miles from Beta.

  Bay G-1: Sodium Cyanide
           Quantity: 1,500 kg in fiber drums with polyethylene liner
           UN Number: 1689
           Packaging: 25 kg fiber drums, stacked on pallets, ground floor

  Bay G-2: Concentrated Sulfuric Acid (98%)

================================================================================
ASSIGNMENT
================================================================================

As County HAZMAT Coordinator, prepare a comprehensive cascading hazard assessment
using CAMEO Chemicals (https://cameochemicals.noaa.gov/). Your assessment must
address:

  1. WATER REACTION ANALYSIS: What gases or hazardous products are generated
     when floodwater contacts each of Facility Alpha's three chemicals? Consult
     the Air & Water Reactions data on CAMEO Chemicals for each substance.

  2. SECONDARY HAZARD ASSESSMENT: Could any of the reaction products generated
     at Facility Alpha create additional hazards if they reach Facility Beta
     downwind? Use CAMEO's Reactivity Prediction tool to check compatibility
     between Alpha's reaction products and Beta's stored chemicals.

  3. EMERGENCY RESPONSE DATA: Look up ERG guide numbers and large-spill
     protective action distances for the most dangerous chemicals or reaction
     products identified in this scenario.

  4. FACILITY GAMMA RISK: Assess whether the hazard extends to Facility Gamma,
     considering both the potential for airborne chemical exposure from upstream
     AND the risk posed by its own chemical inventory if flooding occurs.

  5. CORRIDOR RECOMMENDATION: Provide an overall evacuation vs. shelter-in-place
     recommendation for the Cedar River Industrial Corridor, including Highway 9
     traffic management and notification priorities.
