INCIDENT: Reactor Vessel Explosion and Fire
INCIDENT NUMBER: EHS-2026-CIR-007
DATE/TIME: [Date] 14:22 hours
LOCATION: Pilot Plant Laboratory, Building 4, Bay 7 (Reactor Vessel RV-4)

INJURIES: 2 workers hospitalized (severe chemical burns to hands/face, blast injuries)
           1 worker treated and released (minor eye irritation, ear trauma)
DAMAGE: Reactor vessel RV-4 completely destroyed; significant damage to adjacent equipment;
        structural damage to Bay 7; estimated repair cost $850,000

BACKGROUND PRIOR TO INCIDENT:
On the day of the incident, Process Technicians A and B were assigned to perform a "deep
cleaning" of Reactor Vessel RV-4. The vessel had previously been used for toluene-based
polymer synthesis operations and had been idle for 3 weeks. A visible brownish residue
was present on the interior walls and agitator blades.

The cleaning sequence was NOT covered by any formal Standard Operating Procedure (SOP).
The lead technician devised an improvised cleaning method based on practices they had
observed in other laboratory settings.

WITNESS STATEMENTS (from post-incident interviews):

Technician A (lead): "RV-4 smelled strongly of toluene even though it had been
sitting idle for 3 weeks. We drained what liquid we could and figured we'd use the
nitric acid to do an oxidative clean -- like they do upstairs in the analytical lab
for glassware. I've seen them do it a hundred times and it always works great. Then
we planned to rinse with hydrogen peroxide to make sure everything was oxidized clean."

Technician B: "As soon as the nitric acid went in through the top port, I saw the
liquid immediately start fuming -- yellow-orange fumes. The temperature gauge went
up really fast. We both backed away from the vessel immediately. Maybe 30 seconds
later there was a loud bang and then fire. The explosion blew the viewport glass out."

CHEMICALS FOUND AT OR NEAR INCIDENT SCENE (confirmed by GC analysis and manifest):

Substance 1: TOLUENE RESIDUES (CAS 108-88-3)
  -- Confirmed by GC analysis of reactor drain sample and wall swab
  -- Estimated 2-5 liters of toluene-based polymer solution remained in RV-4

Substance 2: CONCENTRATED NITRIC ACID, ~68% (CAS 7697-37-2, UN 2031)
  -- Partially consumed 20-liter carboy found adjacent to RV-4
  -- Estimated 3-5 liters added to reactor before explosion
  -- Technician A confirmed this was the "oxidative cleaner" they added first

Substance 3: HYDROGEN PEROXIDE 30% (CAS 7722-84-1)
  -- Intact 10-liter bottle found on work cart next to RV-4
  -- Technicians stated they planned to add this "after the acid step"
  -- NOT yet added to reactor at time of explosion

Substance 4: POTASSIUM PERMANGANATE (CAS 7722-64-7)
  -- Small container found in supply cabinet adjacent to Bay 7
  -- Technician A stated they "considered using this instead of peroxide"
  -- NOT used in the cleaning operation

TIMELINE:
14:15  Technicians begin improvised cleaning of RV-4 (no SOP)
14:18  Approximately 3-5 liters of 68% nitric acid added through top port
14:19  Yellow-orange fumes observed; temperature rise noted on gauge
14:22  Explosion -- both technicians clear of direct blast zone (they had backed away)
14:23  Fire suppression activates; fire brigade alerted
