VALUE STREAM MAP (VSM) — CURRENT STATE MAPPING GUIDE
======================================================
Case Study: Toyota Steering Bracket Manufacturing
Reference: Rother, M. & Shook, J. (1998). "Learning to See: Value Stream
           Mapping to Create Value and Eliminate Muda."
           Lean Enterprise Institute, Brookline, MA.
Facility: Acme Stamping (tier-1 supplier to Toyota and Saturn)
Product Family: Left-Hand Steering Bracket

=== CUSTOMER DEMAND ===
Toyota Motor Corp:  12,000 brackets/month  (trays of 20, daily truck delivery)
Saturn Corp:         6,400 brackets/month  (trays of 20, daily truck delivery)
Total demand:       18,400 brackets/month
Working days:       20 per month
Daily demand:          920 brackets/day  (2 shifts × 460 per shift)
Available time/shift:  27,600 seconds (7.67 hours net)
Required Takt Time:    60 seconds/bracket per shift

=== SUPPLIER INFORMATION ===
Supplier: Michigan Steel Coils Inc. (raw steel coil stock)
Delivery: 3× per week (Mon / Wed / Fri)
Order method: Daily EDI orders from Production Control

=== INFORMATION FLOW ===
Toyota & Saturn send: 6-week forecast + weekly firm order via EDI
Production Control: receives orders → issues weekly MRP push schedule to PC Press
All downstream steps: receive daily verbal/paper schedule (non-electronic push)

=== VSM NOTATION REQUIREMENTS (draw.io Lean Mapping Library) ===

USE draw.io's built-in LEAN MAPPING shape library:
  - Manufacturing Process box: one rectangle per process step
  - Inventory Triangle: between each adjacent step (shows WIP units)
  - Push Arrow: straight arrow labeled "PUSH" between steps
  - Supplier icon: factory/building shape on far left
  - Customer icon: factory/building shape on far right
  - Information flow (electronic): lightning bolt / zigzag arrow
  - Information flow (manual): straight arrow with filled arrowhead
  - Production Control box: rectangle at top center
  - Kaizen Burst: starburst/sunburst shape on highest-waste steps
  - Timeline: at the bottom, alternating process time / wait time bars

=== PROCESS DATA TO SHOW IN EACH PROCESS BOX ===
Each process box must contain (read from production_metrics.csv):
  C/T    = Cycle Time in seconds
  C/O    = Changeover time in seconds
  Uptime = Machine Uptime %
  Shifts = shifts per day
  Ops    = operators per shift

=== THE 5 PROCESS STEPS (left to right) ===

Step 1 — PC Press:
  C/T=1s, C/O=3600s, Uptime=85%, 3 shifts, 1 operator
  WIP Inventory triangle AFTER this step: 4,600 units (coil stock)

Step 2 — Spot Weld I:
  C/T=39s, C/O=600s, Uptime=100%, 2 shifts, 1 operator
  WIP Inventory triangle AFTER this step: 4,700 units

Step 3 — Spot Weld II:
  C/T=46s, C/O=600s, Uptime=80%, 2 shifts, 1 operator
  WIP Inventory triangle AFTER this step: 1,100 units

Step 4 — Assembly I:
  C/T=62s, C/O=0, Uptime=100%, 2 shifts, 1 operator
  WIP Inventory triangle AFTER this step: 1,600 units

Step 5 — Assembly II:
  C/T=40s, C/O=0, Uptime=100%, 2 shifts, 1 operator
  WIP Inventory triangle AFTER this step: 1,200 units (finished goods)

Then: SHIPPING (daily truck to Toyota + Saturn)

=== TIMELINE AT BOTTOM ===
Show alternating wait time / process time segments:
  Wait time  = WIP_Inventory / 920 * 86,400 seconds
  Value time = Cycle_Time for each step

  Coil wait:        4,600/920*86,400 = ~432,000 sec  (~5.0 days)  |  PC Press: 1s
  Spot Weld I wait: 4,700/920*86,400 = ~441,000 sec  (~5.1 days)  |  Spot Weld I: 39s
  Spot Weld II wait:1,100/920*86,400 = ~103,000 sec  (~1.2 days)  |  Spot Weld II: 46s
  Assembly I wait:  1,600/920*86,400 = ~150,000 sec  (~1.7 days)  |  Assembly I: 62s
  Assembly II wait: 1,200/920*86,400 = ~113,000 sec  (~1.3 days)  |  Assembly II: 40s
  FG wait:          1,200/920*86,400 = ~113,000 sec  (~1.3 days)

  TOTAL LEAD TIME:        ~23.6 days
  TOTAL VALUE-ADDED TIME: 188 seconds

Label TOTAL LEAD TIME and TOTAL VALUE-ADDED TIME at the right end of the timeline.

=== KAIZEN BURSTS (minimum 3) ===
Place kaizen burst starburst shapes on the TOP 3 worst-waste areas.
Waste = largest WIP inventory = longest wait time.

Top 3 kaizen targets:
  1. PC Press → Spot Weld I gap: 4,600 + 4,700 = 9,300 units WIP total
     Label: "Batch EPE=1 wk — level to daily"
  2. Spot Weld I output: 4,700 units before Spot Weld II (80% uptime bottleneck)
     Label: "Weld II reliability — target 95% uptime"
  3. Assembly I: C/T=62s exceeds takt time of 60s — production bottleneck!
     Label: "Balance line — split task or cross-train"
