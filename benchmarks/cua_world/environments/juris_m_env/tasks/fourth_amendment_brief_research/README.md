# fourth_amendment_brief_research

## Overview
A litigator must build a Fourth Amendment research database in Juris-M from scratch for an appellate brief challenging a warrantless digital search. The library starts with only two irrelevant pre-existing items.

## Goal
1. Add at least 6 Fourth Amendment SCOTUS cases (required: Terry v Ohio, Katz v US, Carpenter v US)
2. Create parent collection 'Fourth Amendment Research' with subcollections 'Favorable Precedent' and 'Adverse Precedent'
3. Assign cases to appropriate subcollections based on whether they support defense (favorable) or government (adverse)
4. Add notes with holdings (>=40 words) to each Fourth Amendment case
5. Tag all Fourth Amendment cases with 'fourth-amendment'

## Scoring
- At least 6 Fourth Amendment cases added: 20 pts
- Terry v. Ohio present: 10 pts
- Katz v. United States present: 10 pts
- Carpenter v. United States present: 10 pts
- Collection hierarchy (parent + 2 subcollections): 15 pts
- Cases assigned to subcollections: 10 pts
- Notes on >=4 Fourth Amendment cases: 25 pts
Pass threshold: 60 points

## Pre-existing items (do not modify)
- Marbury v. Madison, 5 U.S. 137 (1803)
- Brown v. Board of Education, 347 U.S. 483 (1954)
