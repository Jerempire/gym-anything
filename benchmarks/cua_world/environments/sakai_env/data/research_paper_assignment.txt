ASSIGNMENT OVERVIEW
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Write a 5-7 page research paper on a topic related to cell biology. This
assignment requires you to explore a focused area of cell biology in depth,
synthesize information from primary literature, and present your findings in
a clear, well-organized scientific format.

Choose ONE of the following topics:

  1. The role of mitochondria in cellular energy production
     - Explore the chemiosmotic hypothesis, electron transport chain,
       oxidative phosphorylation, and the evolutionary origins of
       mitochondria (endosymbiotic theory).

  2. Cell membrane structure and transport mechanisms
     - Discuss the fluid mosaic model, types of membrane proteins,
       passive and active transport, endocytosis, and exocytosis.
       Include discussion of signal transduction across membranes.

  3. The cell cycle and its regulation
     - Cover the phases of the cell cycle (G1, S, G2, M), cyclin-CDK
       complexes, checkpoint mechanisms, and the consequences of
       cell cycle dysregulation (cancer).

  4. Stem cells and their therapeutic potential
     - Discuss embryonic vs. adult stem cells, induced pluripotent
       stem cells (iPSCs), current therapeutic applications, and
       ethical considerations in stem cell research.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
FORMAT REQUIREMENTS (per MIT OCW 20.109 Guidelines)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Your paper must follow the standard scientific paper format (IMRAD):

TITLE
  - Approximately 10 words; specific to your findings/analysis
  - Should be engaging and informative, not overly broad

ABSTRACT (250 words maximum)
  - Single-spaced, no citations
  - Summarize: motivation, key background, main points of analysis,
    and implications/significance

INTRODUCTION (1-1.5 pages)
  - Structured in approximately three paragraphs:
    Paragraph 1: Broad context and significance of the topic
    Paragraph 2: Narrowing focus to the specific aspect you address
    Paragraph 3: Statement of your paper's specific focus and goals
  - Establish significance and motivate reader interest through
    connections to prior work and real-world applications

BODY / ANALYSIS (2-3 pages)
  - Present your analysis with functional sub-section titles
  - Organize by content/theme, not chronologically
  - Use past tense for established findings
  - Include at least one figure or table with proper caption

DISCUSSION (1-1.5 pages)
  - Interpret findings and connect to the broader field
  - Address limitations of current understanding
  - Propose future directions for research
  - Explain significance and real-world implications

REFERENCES
  - Minimum 5 peer-reviewed references
  - APA format (7th edition)
  - Listed alphabetically by first author's last name
  - Include: all authors, title, journal, year, volume, pages, DOI

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SUBMISSION REQUIREMENTS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  - Length: 5-7 pages (double-spaced, 12pt Times New Roman, 1-inch margins)
  - Submit both:
      (a) An inline text summary (abstract + key findings, 500 words max)
      (b) The full paper as a PDF attachment
  - Due date: Two weeks from assignment posting
  - Late submissions accepted up to one week past due date with 10% penalty
  - File naming: LastName_FirstName_BIO101_ResearchPaper.pdf

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
GRADING RUBRIC (100 points total)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  Content and Scientific Accuracy         30 points
    - Correct understanding of concepts
    - Depth of analysis
    - Integration of primary literature

  Organization and Structure              20 points
    - Logical flow of ideas
    - Appropriate use of IMRAD format
    - Effective use of sub-headings

  Writing Quality                         20 points
    - Clarity and precision
    - Appropriate scientific tone
    - Grammar, spelling, punctuation

  References and Citations                15 points
    - Minimum 5 peer-reviewed sources
    - Proper APA formatting
    - In-text citations used correctly

  Figures/Tables                          15 points
    - At least one figure or table included
    - Proper captions and labeling
    - Referenced in text
