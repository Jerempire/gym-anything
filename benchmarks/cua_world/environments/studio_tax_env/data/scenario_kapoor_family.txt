SECTION 6: NOTES FOR TAX PREPARER — OPTIMIZATION DECISIONS
================================================================================

The following optimization decisions are REQUIRED for this coupled return.
Each decision affects the family's total tax liability.

1. CHARITABLE DONATIONS — POOL ON ARJUN'S RETURN
   Combined family donations: $5,400 + $2,800 = $8,200
   Claim ALL $8,200 on Arjun's return (higher-income spouse).
   Rationale: The first $200 of donations receives a 15% credit; amounts
   above $200 receive a 29% credit (or 33% on income over $235,675).
   Pooling on the higher-income spouse maximizes the value of the credit.

2. MEDICAL EXPENSES — CLAIM ON MEERA'S RETURN
   Total family medical: $6,830 (for all four family members)
   Claim on Meera's return (lower-income spouse).
   Rationale: The medical expense threshold is 3% of net income or $2,759
   (2024 maximum), whichever is less. Meera's net income (~$40,210) yields
   a threshold of ~$1,206. Arjun's net income (~$142,000) yields ~$2,759.
   Lower threshold = more deductible medical expenses.

3. CHILD CARE — MEERA CLAIMS (LOWER-INCOME SPOUSE RULE)
   ITA s.63 requires child care expenses to be claimed by the lower-income
   spouse. Meera's net income ($48,600 employment - RPP - union dues ~
   $42,850) is lower than Arjun's ($155,400 business + investment).
   Claim $8,000 for Anika (capped) on Meera's return.

4. DISABILITY TAX CREDIT — TRANSFER TO ARJUN
   Rohan's DTC ($15,630 base amount) should be transferred to Arjun.
   Arjun has significantly higher federal tax payable, so the non-refundable
   credit is more effectively utilized on his return. The transfer uses
   Schedule 2 (Federal Amounts Transferred from Your Spouse or Common-Law
   Partner) if transferring between spouses, or Line 31800 if from child.

5. CCA ON RENTAL PROPERTIES — DO NOT CLAIM
   Combined rental is a net loss of $2,640. ITA s.1100(11) prohibits
   using CCA to create or increase a net rental loss. Even though CCA is
   technically available on both properties, it MUST NOT be claimed.
   StudioTax may show CCA fields as available — leave them at zero.

6. FORM T2203 — MULTI-PROVINCE ALLOCATION FOR ARJUN
   Arjun has permanent establishments in BC (home office) and AB (rented
   shared office). Allocate business income:
     BC: $126,750 / $195,000 = 65.0%
     AB: $ 68,250 / $195,000 = 35.0%
   Provincial tax is calculated separately on each allocated portion.

7. FORM T657 — LIFETIME CAPITAL GAINS EXEMPTION

   Annual gains limit: Unrestricted (no prior claims)
