# Task: ehr_clinical_risk_stratification

## Domain Context

Accountable Care Organizations (ACOs) and population health programs use risk stratification to identify high-risk patients who need proactive outreach. A Population Health Coordinator reviews risk scores generated by analytics tools and then documents the risk classification in the EHR (as a medical problem) and schedules a care management appointment. This two-step documentation process ensures the care team is alerted and the patient is contacted.

## Occupation / Industry

**Population Health Coordinator** — ACO / Accountable Care Organization / Population Health Management

## Goal

A clinical risk stratification report has been generated by the ACO's analytics platform. The report is located at `/home/ga/Documents/risk_stratification_report.txt`.

Open the report and identify the two high-risk patients flagged for immediate care management intervention. For each patient, perform both required actions:
1. **Document the clinical risk classification** by adding the specified risk problem to the patient's active problem list in LibreHealth EHR
2. **Schedule a care management Office Visit appointment** for the patient

**End state**: Both patients have their risk classification documented in their problem list AND an appointment scheduled in LibreHealth EHR.

## Verification Strategy

- Problem check: queries `lists` table (type='medical_problem'), matches:
  - Patient 1 keyword: "cardiovascular risk"
  - Patient 2 keyword: "uncontrolled type 2 diabetes"
- Appointment check: queries `openemr_postcalendar_events` for count increase vs. baseline (`init_appts` from GT)
- Ground truth in `/tmp/lh_risk_gt.json` with baseline counts
- Requires BOTH actions per patient for full credit on that patient

## Relevant Schema

```sql
-- Medical problems
SELECT pid, title FROM lists WHERE pid = <pid> AND type = 'medical_problem';

-- Appointments
SELECT COUNT(*) FROM openemr_postcalendar_events WHERE pc_pid = <pid>;
```
